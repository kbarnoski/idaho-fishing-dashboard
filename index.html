9:00 PM–DARK
The short, cold window. The sky clears and the wind starts to ease (16→~10 mph) right as the light goes — not the clean calm of the last two nights, but the only real dry shot. If anything rises, throw an Elk Hair / X-Caddis #16 or the BWO #18–20; run a hi-vis indicator dry and set on it dipping. It's getting cold fast (high 40s), so the bite may be brief and stingy — make your few good drifts count, then call it and rest up for the Big Hole.

But wind kills the dry bite, and tonight's wind doesn't quit before dark, so unlike Sun/Tue there's no calm evening payoff — nymph through it. The Ennis valley sites and the Melrose–Divide canyon are dispersal zones with only occasional transient bears. Carry bear spray anyway — you're still in the GYE.
