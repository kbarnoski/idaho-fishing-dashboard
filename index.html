Karel's June 5 Game Plan — South Fork Snake

  • Float fishing is the move. Book a drift boat guide or bring your own rig — 13,600 cfs is too high to wade safely, and flows may still be significant on June 5.
  • Target soft water: cut banks, big eddies, boulder pockets — fish are holding there to escape main current.
  • Prime window is 11 AM–3 PM when water temps warm enough for surface activity. Plan your float accordingly.
  • Pack big bug dries (Stimulator #4–6, Chernobyl #6–8) — Salmonflies may still be trickling and fish eat them aggressively.
  • PMD and Yellow Sally afternoon hatches should be excellent — keep #16–18 Sparkle Duns and Yellow Stimulators within reach.
  • Use heavy tungsten anchor nymphs (Pat's Rubberlegs #6) to get down fast in swift current. Trailer with Juju Baetis #22 or Zebra Midge #20.
  • Crowds will be out — launch from less-trafficked ramps and get on the water early to claim good water ahead of the crowds.
