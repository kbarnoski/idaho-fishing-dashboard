Guide Notes for Your June 5 Trip

  • By June 5, flows should be dropping significantly — the South Fork typically settles into the 5,000–9,000 cfs range in early June, which is the sweet spot for float fishing big dry flies.
  • The salmonfly hatch will be winding down by your trip, but Golden Stones (#6–8) and PMDs (#16–18) will be in full swing — often the best dry fly fishing of the year on the South Fork.
  • Book a guide or have a drift boat lined up — wading access is very limited at any June flow. South Fork Lodge, WorldCast, and Three Rivers Ranch all run excellent guided floats.
  • June mornings in the low-mid 40s°F mean fish wake up slowly. Hit the river by 9 AM and plan a full-day float. Dry fly action typically explodes 11 AM – 4 PM.
  • The Irwin-to-Heise section is a classic full-day float (~14 miles). Heise-to-Lorenzo is shorter and also excellent in June.
  • Bring a full stonefly box — when Golden Stones and PMDs overlap, you'll want both attractor dries and precise match-the-hatch patterns ready to swap.
