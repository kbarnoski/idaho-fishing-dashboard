Tue Jun 23 · Tuned to today (St. Anthony / Vernon): hi ~85°F, near-clear (0% cloud), wind ~18 mph through the afternoon, rain ~1%. Lower Henry's Fork ~1,130 cfs at Rexburg, dropping & clear. 4:00–6:00Late afternoon · warmth + wind = stones
Golden stone / yellow sallie window — and today the wind helps. On a hot, windy day the stoneflies fire as it peaks and the breeze blows them onto the water — the exact setup behind your two Jun 16 browns. Hopper-style foam tight to the banks and out into the seams: Golden Stone foam / Chubby Chernobyl #8–10, a Yellow Stimulator #10–12, or the small Lawson-style stonefly dry that drew the eats above the Vernon ramp. Keep casts short and tucked in the gusts; work the main current too, not just the edges.
6:00–8:00Evening builds · PMD & searching dries
If the wind lays down, fish move up. As the breeze softens toward evening the surface settles and the PMD/searching window opens. PMD Sparkle Dun / CDC Thorax #16 to any noses, with a Parachute Adams #14–16 as the searching attractor over the riffle lines and pod water. Keep the golden-stone foam on a second rod — a warm evening can still draw an opportunistic bank eat. If it's still howling, nymph a Pat's Rubber Legs / PMD nymph dropper through it.
8:00–9:00Spinner fall · the prime hour
The payoff hour. Last light brings the PMD Rusty Spinner #16 fall — fish it dead-drift to the slicks and tailouts. If caddis show (they've been unreliable down low — don't assume it), switch to an Elk Hair Caddis / X-Caddis #14–16. Heads-up tippet, long leader; the fish get pickier as the light flattens.
9:00–dark (~9:45)After-dusk money window
Biggest fish of the night. Once it's nearly dark the Bill's Hole boat-ramp pod has produced your best Vernon fish (the 18" rainbow). Stay on the pod with a caddis or a high-floating Chubby they can find by feel — confidence-fly persistence over a working pod. Fish it till you genuinely can't track the fly.
Weather rule of thumb. Gray/cool day → bugs come early and the dry bite holds longer. Hot/sunny (that's today) → action jams into the last 2 hours of light, so don't fight the bright midday. Stoneflies are warmth-driven — goldens/sallies fire mid–late afternoon on a warm day like this. Wind kills the dry bite — but at ~18 mph it can also deliver stoneflies to the surface (your Jun 16 trigger), so read what it's putting on the water; nymph or fish tucked banks when it's flat-out unfishable. And your own log says the lower-river caddis is flaky right now — lead with stonefly/PMD, treat caddis as a bonus.
