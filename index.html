11:00–2:00 PM
Nymph the pockets — the wind play. Cool front edge keeps the morning slow; punch a short, heavy rig into the boulder seams where the chop breaks the wind. Fly: Pat's Rubber Legs #8–10 with a Zebra Midge / Pheasant Tail #16–18 dropper. A few BWO may show in the gray — not enough to chase on dries in this breeze.
2:00–6:00 PM
PRIME — goldens & yellow sallies fire (warmth-driven). The warmest part of a cool day is your stonefly dry window, and it cuts through wind better than a mayfly. Bang the banks & pocket heads. Fly: foam Golden Stone / Yellow Stimulator #8–12 as the dry, Rubberlegs dropped 18″ below for a dry-dropper that works even if nothing's looking up. Wind-cheat by casting downstream-and-across with a tuck.
6:00–8:30 PM
Evening caddis builds as the day cools and (hopefully) the wind eases. If you've stayed at Box, swing/twitch Elk Hair Caddis #14–16 through the runs. This is also your cue to relocate to Vernon if the breeze has laid down — the lower-river caddis pod is the bigger-fish play once you can present a dry.
8:30–DARK
The money window — caddis blizzard, biggest fish up (your 18″ came right here Friday). Best at Vernon if the wind died; otherwise the Box tailouts. Fly: X-Caddis / Elk Hair #14–16; if heads sip flat water, PMD Rusty Spinner #16. Fish by ear once you can't see the fly.
Weather rule of thumb: Wind kills the dry bite — nymph through it, fish broken water that hides your cast, and wait for the dusk lull before going to delicate dries (today's case). Stoneflies (golden/yellow sally) are warmth-driven and the day's best bet in a breeze — throw the big foam attractor in the warm mid-afternoon, not at dusk. Gray/cool day → BWO & PMD come earlier and the dry bite runs longer; hot/sunny → everything jams into the last 2 hrs of light.
