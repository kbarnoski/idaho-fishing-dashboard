🐻 Grizzly note: Both valley floors are low-risk — bear activity sits in the mountains (Madison Range above Ennis, the upper Big Hole high country), not the open ranch-valley river corridors below. What you're avoiding is the upper Madison near West Yellowstone/Hebgen and inside Yellowstone (core grizzly country). The Ennis valley sites and the Melrose–Divide canyon are dispersal zones with only occasional transient bears. Carry bear spray anyway — you're still in the GYE.

Best wade access is Wise River→Divide; below Divide the river widens and access tightens. Grizzly density very low (first confirmed bear only 2016).

🎣 Slide Inn pilgrimage (Kelly Galloup) — shop stop, not solo wade water: Galloup's Slide Inn sits on the upper Madison “between the lakes” just below Quake Lake — ~45 river miles above Ennis and squarely in core grizzly country (the Hebgen–Quake reach BRF flagged closed for bear activity; the stretch where guide Carl Mock was killed in 2021). No dated report confirms adult salmonflies up there yet — the Jun 7–10 shop reports have the salmonfly only just approaching the lower river, not confirmed at Ennis. Go visit the shop & see the famous water, but keep your wade-and-salmonfly fishing on the valley floor; if you fish up top, don’t go alone in low light and carry spray.

Access & Notes

  • Flat Creek: CLOSED until August 1. Snake at ~9,630 CFS — dropping but still high runoff; guided float only, no wading.
