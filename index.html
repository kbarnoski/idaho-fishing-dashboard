⏱️ Today on the Madison (near Ennis) — when to fish what

Bluebird & breezy: Ennis high ~70°F, ~5% cloud, wind ~15 mph. Water: below Ennis Lake 58.5°F / 1,530 cfs, below Hebgen 51.8°F / 1,010 cfs (provisional). Salmonfly NOT confirmed — everything below is searching/attractor unless a hatch shows. Tune as the day unfolds.
Morningto ~11 AM
Cold overnight (low 38°F) + sunny, so water's slow to wake. Nymph deep along the banks. Pat's Rubber Legs / black-brown stone #6–8 (golden & salmonfly nymphs are migrating bankward) with a PMD nymph or Prince #14–16 dropper. Fish riffle seams and inside corners.
11 AM–2 PMmidday
Sun's high, bite spreads out. Run a hopper-dropper search along grassy banks: Chubby Chernobyl #8–10 over the rubberlegs. BWO unlikely on a 5%-cloud day — don't waste time on small mayflies. Wind starts to build; fish the protected banks.
2–5 PMwarmth window
Golden-stone dry window. Stoneflies are warmth-driven and the goldens are "building" — as the air peaks, skate a yellow Stimulator / Water Walker #8–10 or a yellow sally #14 tight to willows, boulders, and undercut banks. Keep an orange foam salmonfly on as a searching attractor near willows — but no adult salmonflies are reported, so don't expect a blanket eat.
5–7:30 PMcaddis builds
Caddis turn on (best after 4 PM per the shop). Elk Hair Caddis / X-Caddis #14–16 on the swing and dead-drift; swing a soft-hackle pheasant tail through the riffle tailouts as the rises start.
7:30–9:30 PMmoney window
The dusk caddis blizzard + PMD spinner — biggest fish of the day. Spent caddis / X-Caddis #14–16 and a PMD rusty spinner #16 in the film. Fish until you can't see the fly, then one more drift. (Madison sunset ~9:25 PM MDT.)
Weather rule of thumb: Gray/cool day → bugs come early and the dry bite holds longer. Hot/sunny (like today) → action jams into the last 2 hours of light — so don't burn out at midday, save energy for the evening. Stoneflies are warmth-driven and fire mid-afternoon to early evening on warm days. Wind kills the dry bite — with 15 mph gusts, nymph through the chop and hunt the sheltered banks until it lays down toward dusk.
