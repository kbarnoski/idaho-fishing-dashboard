🎣 Today on the Big Hole (Melrose) — when to fish what

Thu Jun 11 · warm & sunny, Twin Bridges 72°F, light wind 7–13 mph easing under 10 by evening — the front has cleared and it's a calm, warm arrival window. You work the morning and drive ~3h, so this is an evening session: figure boots-in around 5–6 PM through dark (sunset ~9:30). Lower/mid river fires first; upper Wisdom still cold (49°F). Rig the night before so you launch straight into the rise.
5:00–7:00 PM
The salmonfly / golden window — warmth-driven, fish it the second you're rigged. A warm sunny day means the adult stones are active into the early evening. Throw a big high-floating dry tight to the grassy banks and overhanging willows — Chubby Chernobyl / Water Walker #4–6 (salmonfly), or a Stimulator / Madame X #6–8 for the goldens. If nothing's eating up top, hang a Pat's Rubber Legs #6–8 dropper 2–3 ft under the foam, or run it under an indicator through the deeper bank seams — the big browns eat the nymph ahead of the dries.
7:00–8:45 PM
The prime evening hatch. As it cools off the peak heat, the caddis #14–16 and a PMD spinner #16 come on over the riffles and tailouts. Keep the stone dry on if fish are still bank-feeding (good searching attractor), but switch to an Elk Hair / X-Caddis #16 or a Parachute PMD #16 to match risers in the softer water. This is the most consistent dry window of the evening.
8:45 PM–DARK
The money window — biggest fish of the night. Last light on a warm calm evening is when the better browns move. Skate or twitch a caddis / October-caddis-size dry near the banks, or if the surface goes quiet, swing a small olive or yellow streamer (Sculpzilla / Sparkle Minnow) through the bank buckets and tailout drop-offs. One good drift in the dark can be the fish of the trip — then call it; you've got full days Fri/Sat.
Weather rule of thumb: Warm & sunny = the stonefly bite is warmth-driven — adults fire mid-afternoon to early evening, so a warm day like today fishes the dry hard right up to dark, with the action jamming into the last 2–3 hours of light. Wind is light tonight so the dry bite holds; if Friday's afternoon gusts kick up, nymph the Rubberlegs through them. Watch for the Fri-night/Sat clouds — a little overcast actually extends the dry-fly window and can trigger a heavier caddis/PMD evening.
