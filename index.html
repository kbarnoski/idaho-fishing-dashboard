8:00–dark (~9:30)Money window
Caddis blizzard — biggest fish. The take that's produced all trip. As the last light goes, the caddis pile up and the better fish commit — Elk Hair Caddis #14–16, with a caddis emerger ~1 ft off the back if they short-take (your Jun 7 lesson). Clear sky = light fades fast and the bite compresses, so a hi-vis post helps you track the fly into the dark. Fish hard till you can't see.
Weather rule of thumb. Gray/cool day → bugs come early and the dry bite holds longer. Hot/sunny → action jams into the last 2 hours of light. Stoneflies are warmth-driven — goldens/sallies fire mid-afternoon on warm days. Wind kills the dry bite — nymph through it and wait for the calm. Today is a clear, calm bluebird evening: no cloud to switch on a Baetis or push the drake, so don't waste it on the meadow sight game — work moving water and pour it into the after-dusk caddis. Then flip the script Saturday: 100% cloud + rain is exactly the day to be on the Ranch chasing the drake — if a shop has confirmed it's reached the caldera.
