🎣 Today on the Big Hole (Maiden Rock–Melrose) — when to fish what

Sat Jun 13 · cool & bright, Twin Bridges 65°F — and windy all day, ~20 mph sustained (gusts to 44 this morning). Day 2 of the salmonfly: adults Glen–Melrose into Maiden Rock, dry edge climbing toward Wise River. Cool + sun + wind = nymph first, dry the afternoon warmth. The chill takes some life out of the adult flight, so lead with a Rubberlegs deep on the banks and ride the foam through the 3–6 PM warm block. Pick the canyon (Maiden Rock–Melrose) for wind breaks; hunt the bank wind-shadows for the dry eats. Below Melrose fish key on dries + egg-layers in the afternoon; above Melrose nymph & streamer. Sunset ~9:30 — but note the wind likely doesn't fully lay down tonight, so the calm-evening caddis blizzard isn't guaranteed.
10 AM–2:30 PM
Morning nymph grind — this is the meat of a cool, windy day. Water's only 54°F at dawn and the wind's already up, so the surface is chopped and the adults are slow to fly — lean into the nymph longer than you did yesterday. Run a Pat's Rubber Legs #6–8 (black/brown) deep on the bank seams and the inside of the canyon corners, with a smaller dropper (Prince / Frenchie #12–14) below. The salmonfly nymphs are migrating to the banks to crawl out, so the eats are right against the edge. On the deeper troughs, an indicator rig of two stone nymphs earns the bigger fish.
2:30–6:30 PM
The salmonfly / golden dry window — warmth-driven, your prime shot on a cool day. The river only nudges into the mid-60s and peaks around 4 PM, so the adults wake up and fly in this block — this is the window to commit to dries, below Melrose where trout key on them. Throw a big high-floater tight to the grassy banks, willows, and any wind-shadow seam — Chubby Chernobyl / Water Walker #4–6 (salmonfly), or a Stimulator / Madame X #6–8 (golden). Hang a Rubberlegs dropper under the foam so a refusal still finds the nymph. When a gust flattens the drift, don't fight it — lob the rig to the next bank pocket and high-stick it. On a cool, breezy day the dry eats may be fewer but they're worth the hunt.
6:30–8:45 PM
Evening hatch — but the wind may stay up tonight. Forecast keeps the breeze at ~17–18 mph even after dark, so don't count on the dead-calm caddis blizzard — play it as it lies. If it does soften, the caddis #14–16 and a PMD spinner #16 come on over the riffles and tailouts; if it stays choppy, keep the big stone dry on as a searching attractor (it rides the chop better) or drop back to the Rubberlegs on the bank seams. Tuck into the most sheltered bend you can find for the cleanest drifts.
8:45 PM–DARK
The money window — biggest fish of the night. Last light, wind dead, caddis thick — this is when the better browns move. Skate or twitch a caddis / October-caddis-size dry near the banks, or if the surface goes quiet, swing a small olive or yellow streamer (Sculpzilla / Sparkle Minnow) through the bank buckets and tailout drop-offs. One good drift in the dark can be the fish of the trip.
Weather rule of thumb: Wind is the headline again today — and wind kills the dry bite, so nymph through it. Keep a Rubberlegs deep on the banks through the chop, then ride the dries when the afternoon warms. Stoneflies are warmth-driven, so on a cool 65°F day the adult/dry action fires later and runs shorter — that 2:30–6:30 PM block is your real window. Tuck into the canyon for wind breaks the open valley won't give you. Tomorrow (Sun) warms to 71°F with the wind easing — better dry conditions, but it's your drift-home day, so the play is to intercept the Madison salmonfly near Ennis on the way south.

📍 Weekend 2 Logistics — Lodging & Low-Grizzly Wade Access

Tap-ready Google Maps links for the Big Hole & Madison salmonfly chase — lodging + park-and-wade spots chosen to stay off core grizzly ground
🐻 Grizzly note: Both valley floors are low-risk — bear activity sits in the mountains (Madison Range above Ennis, the upper Big Hole high country), not the open ranch-valley river corridors below. What you're avoiding is the upper Madison near West Yellowstone/Hebgen and inside Yellowstone (core grizzly country). The Ennis valley sites and the Melrose–Divide canyon are dispersal zones with only occasional transient bears. Carry bear spray anyway — you're still in the GYE.

🎣 Slide Inn pilgrimage (Kelly Galloup) — shop stop, not solo wade water: Galloup's Slide Inn sits on the upper Madison “between the lakes” just below Quake Lake — ~45 river miles above Ennis and squarely in core grizzly country (the Hebgen–Quake reach BRF flagged closed for bear activity; the stretch where guide Carl Mock was killed in 2021). Salmonflies aren’t up there yet either — the leading edge is down here at Ennis. Go visit the shop & see the famous water, but keep your wade-and-salmonfly fishing on the valley floor; if you fish up top, don’t go alone in low light and carry spray.
