The day — June 20, South Fork of the Snake with Rob

“It goes down as the best fishing experience I’ve probably ever had in my life.”

After two dead floats on the lower Henry’s Fork, Rob’s tip from Chris flipped the whole trip: a Heise salmonfly float where the bugs were genuinely on. An epic, fat 18″ cutthroat on a dry — Rob said about as good a fish as you can get on top — plus a 16″ cutthroat with spectacular color, a 16″ brown, a 16″ rainbow, and six smaller cutts off a gravel bar on a PMD. Ten landed, a big rainbow broke you off. That’s the one you’ll be telling for years.

How the trip unfolded

The hatch chase, week by week — what actually happened on the water.

Weekend 1 · Jun 5–8 · Home water, Vernon

Easing in on the lower Henry’s Fork

Straight from the airport to Bill’s Hole — the rose-petal water for Dad — and an 18″ rainbow off a Chubby through a heavy caddis pod on night one. A grinding 10-hour Saturday gave up only a small brown; a short Sunday evening salvaged a 12″ rainbow on a caddis-emerger dropper. Then the eat of the early trip: a thick 16″ brown that turned in the riffle and slammed a golden-stone foam at Bill’s Hole.

5 fish · rainbow + brown · Chubby, caddis emerger, golden-stone foam

Weekend 2 · Jun 11–14 · Big Hole → Beaverhead → Madison

The Montana swing — goldens, a grayling, and a spring creek

Friday, Big Hole: the salmonfly was long gone (Sunrise Fly Shop confirmed a short hatch this year), so the play was goldens. Species variety at Fish Trap — brookies, a rainbow, a grayling, a whitefish — then the find of the day: the Powerhouse diversion channel, where a skated golden-stone dry on the swing drew violent eat after eat. ~11 browns, 12–15″.

Saturday, Poindexter Slough: a new rod from Frontier Anglers and a strong PMD hatch — ~10 technical spring-creek trout, eight from one hole.

Sunday, Madison home: Valley Garden dead (no salmonfly adults, exactly as the dated reports had it), but two browns on a caddis dry up at Lyons Bridge to end the evening honest.

29 fish · brook, rainbow, grayling, whitefish, brown, mixed trout · skated golden stone, small PMD, caddis

Work week · Jun 15–17 · Vernon evenings

The wind, the missing caddis, and two good browns

An odd stretch — the reliable late-evening caddis simply didn’t show three nights running. Monday and Wednesday blanked. But Tuesday, high wind blew stoneflies onto the water above the Vernon ramp and the fish keyed on a Mike Lawson–style small stonefly — two browns, one a powerful 16-incher, before it all shut off.

2 fish · brown · small stonefly dry (wind-driven)

Weekend 3 · Jun 18–22 · The Snake turnaround

Two dead floats, then the day of a lifetime

Mike arrived and Rob lent the driftboat. Thursday Ora–Vernon was slow (one 16″ brown); Friday Ora–Chester was a full skunk — the least bug activity in nine years on that stretch. Then Saturday on the Snake at Heise turned the entire trip around (see above). Monday closed the weekend with a 9-fish South Fork wade — cutthroat on stoneflies then PMDs/caddis, two browns on a swung emerger — the lesson confirmed that the salmonfly eaters were out on boat water you couldn’t reach on foot.

21 fish · cutthroat, brown, rainbow · salmonfly dry, PMD, caddis, stonefly

Final days · Jun 23–26 · The goodbye & “Renegade Day”

A cold run broken — then the best dry-fly day by numbers of your life

A brutal cold run opened the goodbye: skunked on the Harriman Ranch Tuesday evening, then skunked Wednesday chasing the Madison salmonfly at Lyons Bridge on a big golden stone (the bugs were further downriver). No fish since Monday. Thursday at the Ranch a Renegade — an old attractor you’d never fished — drew eat after eat, and on Rob’s afternoon Spring Creek float it put 3 cutthroat in the net and broke the slump. Then Friday, the last day, below Conant: 24 landed up and down one big island riffle, every fish but one on the Renegade — capped by a sighted 16″ fine-spotted cutthroat you called your shot on, beer in hand, and walked down to the gravel bar to land without a net.

27 fish · cutthroat, cutbow, rainbow · the Renegade — see the journal

🎣 Best eat you watched — the Bill’s Hole brown (Jun 8)

What you’ll carry to next year

  • Chase the water that’s fishing, not the hatch you want. Two dead days flipped to the best day of your life the moment you moved rivers. Mobility > grinding.
  • When salmonflies are on big water, the eaters are where only a boat reaches. Wading the side channels got numbers; the float got the salmonfly eats.
  • Skate the golden stone. Swinging it across the current — not a dead drift — triggered violent eats. New go-to when fish are aggressive.
  • Wind can be the trigger. It blew stoneflies onto the water and turned the bite on. Match what the wind delivers.
  • Fish where you can see the eat. The best takes of the trip came in the riffle head in plain sight, not blind in the pool.
  • Trust the dated report over the calendar. Every salmonfly call this trip came down to whether a shop — or the water — actually confirmed adults.
  • Bring the net. Have a car-key spot. The small systems that nearly cost you an 18-incher and a night outside.
